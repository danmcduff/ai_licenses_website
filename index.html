Responsible AI

Responsible AI

The Responsible AI Licensing Intitiative empowers developers to restrict the use of their AI technology by end users and other developers in applications that the creator considers irresponsible or harmful use of their technology.

Imagine you wanted to publish code that could lead to more accurate cancer diagnoses. Could it be applied to powerful surveillance systems?

Imagine you created a fitness tracking app for wearables. Could it be used by insurance providers to increase your premium?

Imagine you built a fun face-swapping filter for your new camera app. Could it be used to create fake news stories?

Licensing Team FAQs

Licenses

We have created simple, legally-enforceable AI Licenses that can be distributed with code or services (including software, APIs, SDKs). These licenses restrict the use of the code or services so that they cannot be misused in healthcare, justice, and media applications. In the absence of laws specifically addressing AI use, we leverage existing copyright law for enforceability.


End User License Agreements

EULAs are aimed for distribution with software tools and datasets.

FAQs

Whose definition of “responsible”? There’s no universal definition of responsible!

We want to provide developers, researchers, and technology providers to release their code/software but retain some leverage about how it might be used.

Why AI and not all CS?

Why are you focusing only these four domain areas (healthcare, justice, media, surveillance)?

Why AI and not all CS?

Recent advances in machine learning and pattern recognition have led to the development of powerful method. These end-to-end methods mean that the same algorithm trained on different data can serve a very different purpose than the developer initially intended.

Why can’t I define my own clauses?

We are looking for community involvement and contributions to the design of these licenses. However, to make them legally robust, the clauses should be carefully designed by legal experts.

Why are there four separate licenses?

Having a different license for each domain provides flexibility of use, so that you can opt in to restrictions in areas where you have concerns about misuse.

Does this license affect any of my rights of ownership or intellectual property rights?

No, the licenses do not affect any of your rights of ownership or usage.
